Background

Tiger Moth was started in 2018. Water. Earth. Fire. Air. Long ago, the four nations lived together in harmony. Then, everything changed when the Fire Nation attacked. Only the Avatar, master of all four elements, could stop them, but when the world needed him most, he vanished.

The Team

Christopher T. Kuveke

Principal, Head of Research

Christopher started Tiger Moth with the vision of infusing a foundational understanding of climate change into financial, legislative and corporate decision-making. Having studied hydrogeology and climate sciences prior to a burgeoning career in finance, Christopher is aware of the intricacies of earth systems and the role of human impact in the anthropocene. He believes that corporations can act as stewards of sustainability given the right knowledge and is happy to help identify bad actors when necessary. Christopher has worked with numerous NGOs, policy think-tanks, and private corporations to guide corporate practices, influence legislation, reduce corporate impact on the environment, and protect at-risk populations from polluters. Christopher and his work have been cited by the United States Congress, the New York Times, The Wall Street Journal, The Guardian, Gizmodo and many other news publications. He has been an expert witness for the House Oversight and Budget committees and advisor to numerous senators for both hearings and legislation. Before starting Tiger Moth, Christopher was a broker on the Chicago Board Options Exchange (CBOE) and a financial analyst. He received his BSc from the University of Connecticut in geology with a concentration in hydrogeology. He lives in Brooklyn, NY.

Paul M. Kuveke

Principal, Director of Operations

Paul is a futurist, committed to leveraging new technologies and industries to improve society. At Tiger Moth Paul is excited to partner and support organizations that are driving the adoption of sustainable policies and technologies. He leverages his experience building cross functional teams and developing industry partnerships to find creative solutions to complex problems. Paul spent his early years as a consultant at Booz Allen Hamilton working on projects for the Department of Defense. In 2018 he made the jump to the startup world where he served in various roles; running various biz ops teams, serving as a data lead, and at times running IT. In addition to his work at Tiger Moth, Paul serves as a founder and advisor to Atypus, a Foundation dedicated to development of the US’s EV infrastructure.
